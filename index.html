and here is a little about myself:

Exchange Program

Coming home from my 1 year exchange program in the United States in 2013/2014, I have had the opportunity to help the travel organization I went with, AFS, in teaching danish teens about different cultural clashes they might face before going abroad.

I also wanted to use my cultural knowledge to help and understand others exchange students in their journey, so I helped my local community arrange social events for foreigners on the program staying in Denmark.

Lastly, I have worked as a liason for a Belgian girl staying at a local danish farm.

I frequently visited her on the farm, and talked about larger or smaller issues that she had.

These experiences have not only given me some extended understanding of other cultures, and extended my teaching skills but have also provided a deeper understanding of the importance of community, and how it helps build the foundation for a good exchange programme.

English Teacher

In the fall of 2017, I have been on another exchange course for 4 months in Myanmar, Cambodia, and Vietnam.

In Myanmar, I attended a school with 20 other Danes,
where we learned about the culture and how to tackle various complicated situations regarding children.

Subsequently, in northern Vietnam, I taught English in a small town called Vị Xuyên for 20 6–7-year-old students.

Later in the process, I chose to transfer to Cambodia's capital Phnom Penh, where I did the same for both small but also medium-sized children. The experience was really great as I got to see a progression in the children, that I taught.

I developed a lot of social skills as I was navigating being a teacher for the first time, and tried to speak with a certain authority and stay cool throughout the lesson.

Army Enlistment

Back in 2018 I voluntarily got drafted for the army in the city of Holstebro.

For me it was all about finding new friends with different backgrounds and trying on new challenges. Throughout my time in the army, I learned about discipline, honor and working together as a team.

When you are given a task by your sergeants in the army, the goal is not to finish first, but to help everyone complete the task and finish as a team.

If one person is not ready, that means that the whole division is not ready. This really opened my eyes to the true meaning of teamwork, and how it can affect the group’s overall spirit.
